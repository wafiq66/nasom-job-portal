Understanding Autism

Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) is a neurodevelopmental condition that affects how people communicate, interact, and process information. Each autistic individual has unique strengths and challenges.

Key Characteristics
Unique Strengths
Attention to detail, pattern recognition, reliability, honesty, and specialized interests
Communication
May prefer direct, clear communication and written instructions
Sensory Processing
May be sensitive to lights, sounds, textures, or other sensory inputs
Routine & Structure
Often thrive with predictable schedules and clear expectations
Why Hire Autistic Employees?
  • Diverse perspectives
  • High attention to detail
  • Reliability and consistency
  • Specialized knowledge
  • Honest communication
  • Strong work ethic

Understanding Autism Levels

Autism is categorized into three levels based on the amount of support needed. Understanding these levels helps create appropriate job opportunities.

Level 1 - Requiring Support
Characteristics:
  • Can work independently
  • May need minor accommodations
  • Good communication skills
  • Can handle social interactions
Suitable Jobs:
  • Data entry
  • Software development
  • Quality assurance
  • Research roles
Level 2 - Requiring Substantial Support
Characteristics:
  • Needs moderate support
  • May have communication challenges
  • Benefits from structured environments
  • May need social support
Suitable Jobs:
  • Assembly work
  • Packaging
  • Sorting tasks
  • Repetitive tasks
Level 3 - Requiring Very Substantial Support
Characteristics:
  • Needs significant support
  • Limited verbal communication
  • May have sensory sensitivities
  • Requires specialized support
Suitable Jobs:
  • Simple repetitive tasks
  • Assembly line work
  • Cleaning tasks
  • Supported employment

Creating Inclusive Job Advertisements

Use our job posting form to create comprehensive and inclusive job advertisements that attract autistic applicants.

Essential Job Ad Components

  • Professional Jobs: Require higher education, complex problem-solving
  • Semi-Skilled Jobs: Moderate training, routine tasks with some variety
  • Simple Jobs: Basic training, repetitive tasks, clear instructions

  • Level 1: Minimal support needed, independent work
  • Level 2: Moderate support, structured environment
  • Level 3: Significant support, specialized assistance

  • Noise Levels: Specify if quiet environment is available
  • Lighting: Mention if adjustable lighting is provided
  • Space: Describe workspace and privacy options
  • Flexibility: Explain schedule and work arrangement options
Interview & Support Information
Interview Accommodations:
  • Written questions in advance
  • Quiet interview space
  • Support person present
  • Extra time for responses
Workplace Support:
  • Mentor or work buddy
  • Regular progress updates
  • Clear communication channels
  • Flexible work arrangements

Best Practices for Hiring Autistic Employees

During Recruitment
  • Use clear, direct language in job ads
  • Provide detailed job descriptions
  • Offer multiple application methods
  • Give advance notice for interviews
  • Provide interview questions beforehand
During Interview
  • Create a quiet, comfortable environment
  • Allow extra time for responses
  • Ask specific, direct questions
  • Focus on skills and abilities
  • Be patient and understanding
Onboarding
  • Provide written instructions
  • Assign a mentor or buddy
  • Create a structured training plan
  • Establish clear expectations
  • Provide regular feedback
Ongoing Support
  • Maintain open communication
  • Provide regular check-ins
  • Offer flexibility when possible
  • Recognize and celebrate achievements
  • Create an inclusive workplace culture
